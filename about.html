About PolicyRadar

Our Mission

PolicyRadar was created to solve a common problem faced by professionals, students, and citizens interested in Indian policy developments: information overload.

Every day, important policy news is scattered across dozens of sources‚Äîgovernment websites, newspapers, think tanks, and specialized publications. Staying informed requires scanning multiple websites, newsletters, and social media feeds, often leading to missed information or overwhelming reading lists.

PolicyRadar cuts through this noise by carefully curating the most significant policy developments across key domains. We monitor over 30 trusted sources so you don't have to, bringing you a concise, organized view of what matters in Indian policy.

Our Curation Process

Each policy update on PolicyRadar passes through a deliberate selection and summarization process:

  1. Comprehensive Monitoring: We track official government communications, major news outlets, specialized policy publications, and respected think tanks.
  2. Significance Filtering: We select stories based on their potential impact, relevance to current debates, and long-term importance.
  3. Clear Categorization: Each story is organized by policy domain and tagged by content type (legislation, analysis, court ruling, etc.).
  4. Key Points Extraction: We identify and highlight the most important elements of each development.
  5. Context Addition: Where appropriate, we provide brief notes on why a particular development matters.

All content links to original sources, allowing you to explore topics in greater depth when needed.
