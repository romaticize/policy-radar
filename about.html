About PolicyRadar

India's policy intelligence platform

PolicyRadar tracks Indian policy developments across 240+ government, regulatory, and media sources. Every day, it aggregates, classifies, and surfaces the policy actions that matter — from RBI circulars to Supreme Court orders to Parliament committee reports.

The goal is simple: make it possible for anyone — journalists, researchers, businesses, citizens — to understand what the Indian government is actually doing, without having to monitor hundreds of sources manually.

How it works

PolicyRadar runs a fully automated daily pipeline:

240+ sources fetched
→
Articles classified
→
Deduplicated & clustered
→
Published to site

Sources include government websites (RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, PIB, MoF, and dozens more), major news outlets, legal publications, and policy think tanks. Articles are classified into policy categories using a combination of language models and semantic analysis.

What PolicyRadar is not

PolicyRadar is an aggregator and classifier, not an editorial outlet. It does not produce original reporting, offer legal or financial advice, or represent any government body. Article summaries are generated from source content — always refer to the original source for full details.
