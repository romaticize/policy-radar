RBI policy: 'Inflation target sacrosanct for us, not in abeyance at all'
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) maintained its repo rate at 5.25% and kept its policy stance neutral during the June 2026 monetary policy meeting. The RBI emphasized that its inflation target remains a priority, while also introducing measures to attract foreign exchange. Deposit and lending rates have risen despite the rate pause.

LPG price hike: Despite increase, OMCs incurring under-recovery of Rs 700/cylinder, says government
Multiple sources report that the Indian government has raised LPG cylinder prices by ₹29 per cylinder due to global cost surges, despite incurring under-recovery of ₹700 per cylinder. The government defended the hike, stating that India still has among the lowest LPG prices in the world, while the TMC criticized the "Vishwaguru" government for failing to protect people from price shocks.

Shri Swaminathan Janakiraman re-appointed as RBI Deputy Governor
The government has re-appointed Swaminathan Janakiraman as RBI Deputy Governor for two years. RBI Governor Sanjay Malhotra has stated that differential deposit rates beyond RBI norms are not acceptable and that the proposal for polymer currency notes is under consideration.

Capital flow push: RBI eases foreign borrowing rules for PSUs, FCNR deposits of banks
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) announced multiple measures to attract foreign capital, including easing foreign borrowing rules for public sector undertakings (PSUs) and relaxing norms for FCNR deposits of banks. Additionally, India's foreign exchange reserves increased by $938 million to reach $682 billion, as reported by the RBI.
