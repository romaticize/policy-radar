The Iran Peace Deal and India's Energy Reset

June 15 – 21, 2026
The Week in One Paragraph

The US-Iran peace pact dominated this week, reshaping India's energy calculus as oil slid below $80, the Strait of Hormuz reopened, and the RBI's MPC minutes revealed West Asia uncertainty as the central reason behind its rate pause. Jio Platforms filed its DRHP for what would be India's largest-ever IPO. Modi's G7 engagements produced an India-UK trade pact activation date of July 15, intel-sharing talks with Canada, and a final push toward an India-US trade deal. Domestically, the TMC rebellion deepened alongside a near-split in Shiv Sena (UBT), while India secured the FATF Vice Presidency for the first time.

Deep Dive

The Iran Peace Deal and India's Energy Reset

What Happened

After 108 days of conflict that choked the Strait of Hormuz and pushed crude past $100 a barrel, the US and Iran signed an interim peace agreement on June 18, setting a 60-day window for a permanent settlement. By June 19, Brent crude had fallen below $80 — heading for a 9% weekly loss — and the first Indian LNG carrier, Disha, had transited the Strait and docked at Gujarat's Dahej port. The crisis, tracked across multiple escalations since March, had forced India to diversify crude sourcing and surge Russian oil imports as a stopgap.

Why It Matters

Energy and the current account

India imports over 85% of its crude, with the Gulf supplying roughly 40% before the conflict. The Hormuz reopening is expected to ease shipping costs and stabilise LNG supply — though experts caution that energy prices are unlikely to return to pre-conflict levels. The government has directed ONGC to build a Rs 15,000 crore strategic oil reserve at Mangaluru, signalling that the vulnerability exposed by the crisis will outlast the crisis itself.

Monetary policy

The RBI MPC minutes, released June 20, showed that West Asia conflict, crude spikes, and monsoon risks dominated deliberations. The rate pause was driven by fears of a policy misstep amid geopolitical uncertainty. With oil now falling, the RBI has more room to consider easing — but the minutes suggest it will wait for durable stability.

Trade and exports

Industry expects a rebound in basmati rice exports to the Gulf and a broader recovery in India's trade performance with West Asia, a region that had seen significant disruption during the conflict.

Rupee and consumers

The rupee strengthened sharply, touching 94.29 against the dollar. A sustained crude decline would lower costs for petroleum-derived raw materials — from plastics to agricultural inputs — with consumer pass-through over the coming months.

What's Next

The 60-day negotiating window sets an August deadline for a permanent settlement. Three Indian crude supertankers have reappeared in the Gulf of Oman. But Iran and the US are already trading conflicting claims over Hormuz's future governance, and regional energy infrastructure will take time to recover.

Bottom Line: India just got a macroeconomic breather — but 108 days of Hormuz disruption have permanently changed how Delhi thinks about energy security.
Five More Stories That Matter
Story 1

Jio Platforms Files for India's Biggest-Ever IPO

Reliance Industries' digital arm filed its DRHP with SEBI on June 19, planning a fresh issue of 27 crore equity shares — no offer-for-sale, meaning all proceeds flow into the company. Estimates peg the raise at $3–4 billion, which would make it India's largest-ever listing. Proceeds are earmarked primarily for debt reduction (~Rs 27,500 crore), with the remainder funding AI infrastructure. The filing marks a generational transition: Akash Ambani now leads as managing director, and the IPO will be a pure fundraise with no investor exits.

Story 2

India-UK Trade Pact Set for July 15 After Steel Row Resolved

After months of negotiations that nearly collapsed over UK steel safeguard measures, the India-UK Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement will enter force on July 15. The pact eliminates tariffs on 99% of India's exports and extends social security exemptions for Indian professionals from three to five years. India secured protection for 85% of its steel exports from the UK's new quota system. Signed in July 2025, the deal's implementation was repeatedly delayed by the steel dispute, ultimately resolved at the G7.

Story 3

Modi's G7 Diplomacy: Trade Push, India-Canada Reset, Seafarer Safety

Prime Minister Modi used G7 sideline meetings to advance several diplomatic tracks. The Modi-Trump meeting signalled a final push for an India-US trade deal. With Canada's Carney, Modi launched intel-sharing pact talks and both committed to concluding a trade agreement by year-end. Modi also raised Indian seafarer safety — a pointed reference to the Iran conflict's toll on shipping crews. The week began with Modi becoming India's longest-serving PM on June 15, a milestone he leveraged into this concentrated burst of summitry.

Story 4

TMC Rebellion and Shiv Sena (UBT) Near-Split Reshape Opposition

Two parallel political crises accelerated. In Bengal, Abhishek Banerjee urged Speaker Birla to disqualify 20 rebel TMC MPs, while Mamata Banerjee filed an election petition challenging Suvendu Adhikari's win. In Maharashtra, six Shiv Sena (UBT) MPs skipped a party meeting and reportedly headed to Delhi with Eknath Shinde, prompting Sanjay Raut to allege MPs were offered Rs 50 crore to switch sides. Uddhav Thackeray offered to resign as party president. Both rebellions have been escalating since early June — potentially handing the BJP significant parliamentary gains.

Story 5

BrahMos Goes Global: Russia Seeks Induction, New Variants Announced

India and Russia announced joint development of four new BrahMos missile variants — smaller, faster, and with longer range — while Russia expressed interest in inducting the cruise missile into its own forces. Separately, DRDO successfully flight-tested its indigenous Long Range Land Attack Cruise Missile off Odisha on June 15. This represents a significant shift: India moving from buyer to potential supplier in the defence relationship with Russia, with a Vietnam deal also near finalisation.

Sector Spotlight: Finance

Finance was the third most active category this week, but its real significance lies in the breadth of RBI action. Beyond the MPC minutes, the central bank issued second amendment directions on responsible business conduct for commercial banks, payment banks, and local area banks. It also revised capital adequacy norms for regional rural banks and cooperatives, updated Lead Bank Scheme guidelines, waived collateral for agricultural loans up to Rs 2 lakh, and issued deposit rate amendment directions. RBI coverage has been consistently heavy since March, reflecting a central bank actively restructuring India's banking framework. Separately, Vivek Aggarwal's election as the first Indian FATF Vice President marked a milestone in India's role in global financial governance.

On Our Radar

India-EU FTA by December. Commerce Minister Goyal confirmed an India-EU trade pact is on track for signing by year-end, with the EU's von der Leyen reinforcing the commitment at the G7. With the India-UK CETA now activated, India may have two major Western market FTAs in force within 12 months.

Telegram ban and NEET re-exam. The government temporarily banned Telegram ahead of the NEET-UG re-examination on June 21, citing paper-leak risks. Delhi High Court upheld the ban through June 22 — a significant precedent for platform-level restrictions around examinations.

Windfall tax recalibration. The government hiked export duties on diesel and ATF while keeping petrol unchanged — the latest fortnightly adjustment since the Hormuz crisis. With oil prices now falling, the next revision will test whether Delhi eases duties to support refinery margins.

India's inflation measurement overhaul. On June 15, the government launched a revised Wholesale Price Index with base year 2022-23 alongside new Producer Price Indices — beginning India's transition from WPI to PPI, aligning with IMF global standards. The WPI will be phased out over five years.

Naval tri-commissioning in Kolkata. PM Modi commissioned three indigenously built warships on June 21 — including the stealth frigate INS Dunagiri, armed with BrahMos missiles — the first-ever tri-commissioning from a single shipbuilder.

Karnataka HC declares Gameskraft founders' arrest illegal. The High Court ruled that the ED's arrest of three Gameskraft founders under the PMLA was unlawful, ordering immediate release — reinforcing Supreme Court precedent on strict procedural compliance for arrests under anti-money laundering law.
